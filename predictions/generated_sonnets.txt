Those lips that Love's own hand did make
Breathed forth the sound that said "I hate"
To me that languished for her sake;
She did not wish that I should perish,
For she loved not that I should die.
But, for love's sake, I would die for love's sake,
For love's sake she hated me.
So she did not find that I did not love;
For she saw that I was still alive.
   And now I am in heaven with love's death,
  And in heaven she lives still with me.

Poor soul, the center of my sinful earth,
Pressed with these rebel powers that thee array,
Why dost thou pine within and suffer dearth,
Then wouldst thou do my pleasure's sake,
Who dost thou weep of me to be told,
Or that I might be brought to thee,
Who with the ease of thy voice would love thee to thy soul,
If thou wouldst not break me up with thy tears,
And in thy sight be thy breast, of thy garments I might not lie.
  This I think thou wilt to do,
  Where I am not thy loving mistress,
  For thou dost tell me what thou lovest.


2
My love is as a fever, longing still
For that which longer nurseth the disease,
Feeding on that which doth preserve the ill,
Which, in the young man's face, grows bitter
To be the proud star of the age,
Which sooth no longer to be its lover's tender,
Nor to be seen by the sad eyes of his state
Which now bear the burden of his self-conciliation.
  Yet for my love, I can die alone,
  To live where I live and live where I die.
  My love's true love, my love's true love,
  I may live in any place where I live,
  Where I love and live and live.


3
O me, what eyes hath love put in my head,
Which have no correspondence with true sight!
Or if they have, where is my judgment fled,
That I can not see my nature on?
Or if they have, where is my mind,
Which is no use to me,
Or what doth my eye do in my mind?
  If I am not the mind of love,
  I am the eye of truth.
  For love, I love thee, thou art my sweetest self.

5
O, from what power hast thou this powerful might
With insufficiency my heart to sway?
To make me give the lie to my true sight,
I am forced to say, from this power,
That I should most love the eye,
And to thy influence, from this ill-doing,
And that I should best serve thy part.
  For if I had not, by that means,
  The lust of my eyes, which I should feel,

  My true love would not be true,
  And yet I have now seen how it is so,

6
Love is too young to know what conscience is;
Yet who knows not conscience is born of love?
Then, gentle cheater, urge not my amiss,
For love's gentle stamp on your heart's bones is too weak,
For that gentle stamp, which is not broken,
That it lies concealed within you,
In my countenance, and in the stain that it carries,
Grows in your heart, and gushes out love's secret,
Which you cannot contain.
  I hate thee, dear love, and I love thee,
  For my love's pleasure is not the heart of thy heart.

In loving thee thou know'st I am forsworn,
But thou art twice forsworn, to me love swearing;
In act thy bed-vow broke, and new faith torn
To this same false thyself,
And thou wilt have it now.
I, on the contrary, am thy love's proud bed,
And am thou a new gift of thy own wit,
And am thy woe to this fool's house
Thy ass-conjurer and thy sweet beauty.
  I am love's ransom for thy new faith;
  I am not old, nor am I guilty;
  But thou art my sweet, and I am thy love's sweet ransom.

The little love-god, lying once asleep,
Laid by his side his heart-inflaming brand,
Whilst many nymphs that vowed chaste life to keep
And love to his love, still he could not hear
His plea, but his love's tears:
He died, for love's sake, for love's sake's sake,
And when he died, he heard no cry;
Yet love's tears and their cries were the same,

  Thus my dear old love died, and he died with him.

When my love swears that she is made of truth,
I do believe her, though I know she lies,
That she might think me some untutored youth,
That I might love her with a happy heart,
Or if she should seem to be so,

11
Two loves I have, of comfort and despair,
That like two spirits do suggest me still.
My better angel is a man right fair,
Which in his love, not wanting to lose his true love,
May love have his other side by his side,
And love in his love to the wrong end may be true.
So my love, then, is fair, and fair is fair.
  If that be true, my love is fair.
  So, fair as fair is fair,
  I love those who love me,
  And those who love me, are fair.
