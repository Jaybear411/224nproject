Knowing thy heart torment me with disdain,
Have put on black, and loving mourners be,

Beshrew that heart that makes my heart to groan
For that deep wound it gives my friend and me.
Is 't not enough to torture me alone,
Nor am I to curse my neighbor,
Nor am I to mock him for his love.
Nor am I to wail in my ear,
Nor do I think of myself more truly in love than thou hast given me.
But what's worse, if my love's nature is not his,
What's worse is that I see him like this,
That all thy thoughts can hear.
   But I love him so much that I do love him,
  And yet my heart shall not wail of him,
  And it will not weep for him with me.

O, call not me to justify the wrong
That thy unkindness lays upon my heart;
Wound me not with thine eye but with thy tongue;
Acknowledge me that I am a boy, and by thee

8

And thy part I am to blame.

Lo, as a careful huswife runs to catch
One of her feathered creatures broke away,
Sets down her babe, and makes all swift dispatch.
But now I, thou, my daughter,
Where thou, with my breast, findst thy way back

So now, my dear friend, take heed
That thou shalt not cross the lines of thy beauty
And thy beauty's side.
  I, thou, mayst prove thy worth by my grace,
  For I, with thee, give thee strength to hold.
